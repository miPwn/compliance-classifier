1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 Purpose
This Code of Conduct and Ethics ("Code") establishes the standards of ethical business conduct expected from all employees, officers, and directors of ACME Corporation and its subsidiaries ("ACME" or the "Company").

1.2 Scope
This Code applies to all employees, officers, and directors of ACME Corporation and its subsidiaries worldwide. We also expect our suppliers, contractors, consultants, and other business partners to adhere to similar ethical standards when working on our behalf.

1.3 Responsibility
Each individual covered by this Code is responsible for:
- Reading, understanding, and complying with this Code
- Seeking guidance when in doubt about the proper course of action
- Reporting known or suspected violations of the Code
- Cooperating in investigations of possible violations

2. CORE VALUES AND PRINCIPLES

Our business conduct is guided by the following core values and principles:

2.1 Integrity
We act with honesty and integrity in all our business dealings and relationships.

2.2 Respect
We treat all individuals with dignity and respect, valuing diverse perspectives and experiences.

2.3 Accountability
We take responsibility for our actions and decisions, and we hold ourselves accountable to our stakeholders.

2.4 Excellence
We strive for excellence in everything we do, delivering high-quality products and services.

2.5 Innovation
We encourage creative thinking and innovative solutions to meet the evolving needs of our customers.

3. WORKPLACE CONDUCT AND EQUAL OPPORTUNITY

3.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
ACME is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all qualified individuals without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

3.2 Diversity and Inclusion
We value diversity and strive to create an inclusive environment where all employees can contribute fully to the Company's success.

3.3 Professional Conduct
Employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally and treat others with respect and dignity. Unprofessional behavior, including offensive language, intimidation, or disrespectful treatment of others, is not tolerated.

4. ANTI-HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION

4.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
ACME has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment and discrimination. Harassment includes any unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

4.2 Sexual Harassment
Sexual harassment, including unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature, is strictly prohibited.

4.3 Reporting Harassment or Discrimination
Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should report it immediately to their supervisor, Human Resources, or through the Ethics Hotline.

5. HEALTH AND SAFETY

5.1 Safe Work Environment
ACME is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees. We comply with all applicable health and safety laws and regulations.

5.2 Employee Responsibilities
Employees are responsible for following safety procedures, reporting unsafe conditions, and participating in safety training programs.

5.3 Substance Abuse
Working under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs is prohibited. The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs on Company premises is strictly forbidden.

6. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

6.1 Definition
A conflict of interest occurs when an individual's private interest interferes, or appears to interfere, with the interests of the Company. Conflicts of interest can arise in various situations, including:
- Personal relationships with suppliers, customers, or competitors
- Outside employment or business activities
- Financial investments in competitors, suppliers, or customers
- Corporate opportunities

6.2 Disclosure
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their supervisor or the Legal Department.

6.3 Recusal
Employees should recuse themselves from decisions where they have a conflict of interest.

7. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

7.1 Protection of Confidential Information
Employees must protect confidential information about the Company, its customers, suppliers, and other business partners. Confidential information includes:
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Financial data and business plans
- Customer and employee information
- Research and development activities
- Pricing and marketing strategies

7.2 Use of Confidential Information
Confidential information should be used only for legitimate business purposes and should not be disclosed to unauthorized individuals.

7.3 Intellectual Property
Employees must respect and protect the Company's intellectual property, including patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets.

8. PROTECTION AND USE OF COMPANY ASSETS

8.1 Proper Use of Assets
Company assets should be used only for legitimate business purposes. Employees are responsible for protecting Company assets from theft, waste, and misuse.

8.2 Electronic Communications
Company email, internet, and other electronic communication systems should be used primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted as long as it does not interfere with job responsibilities.

8.3 Information Security
Employees must follow information security policies and procedures to protect Company data and systems from unauthorized access or cyber threats.

9. FAIR DEALING

9.1 Fair Competition
ACME competes vigorously but fairly. We do not engage in unfair or deceptive business practices.

9.2 Relationships with Stakeholders
Employees should deal fairly with customers, suppliers, competitors, and other stakeholders. We do not take unfair advantage of anyone through manipulation, concealment, abuse of privileged information, misrepresentation, or any other unfair dealing practice.

10. COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS AND REGULATIONS

10.1 Legal Compliance
ACME is committed to complying with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations in the countries where we operate.

10.2 Insider Trading
Employees who have access to material, non-public information about the Company or other companies are prohibited from using or sharing that information for stock trading purposes.

10.3 Antitrust and Competition Laws
Employees must comply with antitrust and competition laws, which prohibit anti-competitive agreements and practices.

11. ANTI-CORRUPTION AND BRIBERY

11.1 Zero Tolerance for Bribery
ACME prohibits bribery in all business dealings, in every country, with both governments and the private sector. Employees must not offer, give, solicit, or accept bribes, kickbacks, or other improper payments.

11.2 Facilitation Payments
Facilitation payments, even where legal, are discouraged and require prior approval from the Legal Department.

11.3 Third Parties
Employees must ensure that third parties acting on behalf of the Company do not engage in bribery or corruption.

12. GIFTS AND ENTERTAINMENT

12.1 Acceptable Gifts and Entertainment
Modest gifts and entertainment may be given or received if they:
- Are not cash or cash equivalents
- Are consistent with customary business practices
- Are not excessive in value
- Cannot be construed as a bribe or payoff
- Do not violate applicable laws or regulations

12.2 Government Officials
Stricter rules apply to interactions with government officials. Consult the Legal Department before providing any gift or entertainment to a government official.

13. POLITICAL ACTIVITIES AND CONTRIBUTIONS

13.1 Personal Political Activities
Employees may participate in political activities on their own time and at their own expense. Personal political views and activities should not be represented as those of the Company.

13.2 Corporate Political Contributions
Corporate political contributions must comply with applicable laws and require approval from the Legal Department.

14. ENVIRONMENTAL RESPONSIBILITY

14.1 Environmental Compliance
ACME is committed to complying with all applicable environmental laws and regulations.

14.2 Sustainable Practices
We strive to minimize our environmental impact through sustainable business practices, including energy conservation, waste reduction, and responsible resource management.

15. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

15.1 Reporting Channels
Employees should report known or suspected violations of this Code to:
- Their supervisor
- Human Resources
- The Legal Department
- The Ethics Hotline (anonymous reporting available)

15.2 Investigation Process
All reports will be promptly investigated. The Company will maintain confidentiality to the extent possible while conducting a thorough investigation.

16. NON-RETALIATION POLICY

16.1 Protection from Retaliation
ACME prohibits retaliation against any employee who, in good faith, reports a known or suspected violation of this Code or participates in an investigation.

16.2 Consequences for Retaliation
Any employee who engages in retaliation will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

17. DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. In some cases, violations may also lead to civil or criminal penalties.

18. WAIVERS AND AMENDMENTS

18.1 Waivers
Any waiver of this Code for executive officers or directors may be made only by the Board of Directors and will be promptly disclosed as required by law or stock exchange regulations.

18.2 Amendments
This Code may be amended from time to time by the Board of Directors. Amendments will be communicated to all employees.

ACKNOWLEDGMENT

I acknowledge that I have received and read the ACME Corporation Code of Conduct and Ethics. I understand the standards and policies contained in the Code and understand that there may be additional policies or laws specific to my job. I agree to comply with the Code in all respects.

If I have questions concerning the meaning or application of the Code, any Company policies, or the legal and regulatory requirements applicable to my job, I know I can consult my supervisor, Human Resources, or the Legal Department, knowing that my questions or reports to these sources will be maintained in confidence.
